Outcomes

The overall outcomes collected at the individual level within the period covered by the data pull included:

  • welcome Whether the person had a welcome meeting
  • uiexit Whether the person stopped making unemployement claims for 3 weeks in a row.
  • employed Whether a different company reports wages for the person in a quarter after the quarter they applied for UI
  • time.to.exit The number of days between making the first claim and exiting the program.
  • plans Number of action plans uploaded by an individual in the program
  • wages The wages earned by the person (?average? ?weekly?)

Effects Excluding Those With a Short Time on the Program

A small portion of the cases appear to have negative times to exiting the program, (N=957 or 2.3 percent of cases)

Time to exiting the program is calculated by the subtracting participants initial unemployment claim date from the date they exited the unemployment insurance. An individual is considered to have exited the program after three weeks of not claiming benefits. Thus exit dates are always on Sundays when a new claim period begins, while initial claim date may be any day during the week. In fact nearly 87.6 percent of these cases have values implying exit dates within seven days of the claim process, suggesting most of the negative cases are due to disconnect between how claims are processed and exit times are calculated. Still, as a robustness check, we repeat the main analysis, removing participants with negative times to exit. The results are essentially unchanged.

Effects on Those With More Time in the Program?

One idea is that effects of the program might be smaller among those who have had little time in the program. So, we might inspect the effects among those who have been in the program longer (say, those with BYE<=201718 (Benefits Year End Date of the 18th week of 2017).

First, however, before we divide up the sample in this way, we should see if there are differential effects of treatment on BYE. Even if we find that the treated and control groups are balanced on this, if we think that the treatment may have a causal effect on this factor, then, below, we may want to do a different kind of analysis (i.e.consider BYE as the dose and random assignment as the instrument).
